Art of Tea Making...

From Lush Green to Your Cuppa

Withering

Fresh tea leaves are spread out to lose moisture up to 40%-45% and soften, enhancing flavor.

READ MORE

Rolling

The withered leaves are rolled to break cell walls, releasing enzymes aiding oxidation and shaping.

READ MORE

Fermentation & Drying

The leaves oxidize and are then heated to halt oxidation, preserving flavor and aroma.

READ MORE

Sorting & Grading

After drying, tea is sorted by size and quality. Grading determines price and flavor profile.
