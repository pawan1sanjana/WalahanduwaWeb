Art of Tea Making...

From Lush Green to Your Cuppa

Withering.

Fresh tea leaves are spread out to lose moisture upto 40%-45% and soften and enhances the flavor.

READ MORE

Rolling.

The withered leaves are rolled to break the cell walls, releasing enzymes that help in oxidation and shape the leaves.

READ MORE

Fermentation & Drying.

The leaves oxidize, then are heated to stop oxidation and preserve their taste and aroma.

READ MORE

Sorting and Grading

After drying, the tea is sorted by size and quality. Grading classifies the tea, impacting its price and taste.
